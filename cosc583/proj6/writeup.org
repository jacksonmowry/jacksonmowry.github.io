* A textual summary of any interesting differences or common features that you observe.
While most of the servers tested here follow a very similar configuration pattern it was interesting to see how the grades across services vary. For the majority of servers that did not receive an A+ it seems that SSL Labs does not like when a server supports older versions of TLS such as 1.0 and 1.1. Those services which do support the most recent version of TLS (1.3) SSL Labs gives a bump in grade. This seems to be a careful balance of supported modern protocols, while deprecating older ones as soon as your user base begins phasing those protocols out.

For those services which support TLS 1.3 they have almost identical configurations, settling on AES with 128 bit keys, and GCM for the mode. This makes sense as if most of the web supports a specific protocol it will be necessity have the most eyes on attempting to poke holes in the code.

I also found it interesting that large companies (and those who provide cloud hosting) tend to have shorter certificate chains where they are also found within the chain.

Lastly I am interested in how these companies model the threat of long lived certificates, as I know client companies like Apple and Google are pushing for these certificates to be shorter and shorter.
